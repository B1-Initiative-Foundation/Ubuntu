Here is a concise biography of Wesley Snipes:

## Personal Life
Wesley Snipes was born on July 31, 1962 in Orlando, Florida to Marian Long, a teacher's assistant, and Wesley Rudolph Snipes, an aircraft engineer. [2][3] He grew up in the Bronx, New York and attended the High School of Performing Arts in New York City before moving back to Florida. [2][3] Snipes has been married twice - first to April Dubois from 1985 to 1990, and then to Nakyung Park since 2003. He has six children. [2]

## Career
Snipes began his acting career in the 1980s, making his film debut in the 1986 movie "Wildcats". [2][3] He gained recognition for his roles in films like "Mo' Better Blues" (1990), "New Jack City" (1991), and "Jungle Fever" (1991). [3] Throughout the 1990s, Snipes starred in numerous action and thriller films such as "Passenger 57" (1992), "Rising Sun" (1993), "Demolition Man" (1993), and the "Blade" superhero franchise (1998-2004). [2][3] He also appeared in comedies like "To Wong Foo, Thanks for Everything! Julie Newmar" (1995). [3] In the 2000s, Snipes had smaller roles in direct-to-video films before returning to major theatrical releases in the 2010s. [2][3] He has also worked as a producer through his production company Amen-Ra Films. [2][4]

## Achievements
Snipes has been nominated for an Independent Spirit Award for Best Supporting Male for his role in "The Waterdance" (1992). [2][3] He is a 5th dan black belt in Shotokan Karate and a 2nd dan black belt in Hapkido, and many of his action films have featured his martial arts skills. [2] In 2017, Snipes made his debut as a novelist with the urban fantasy novel "Talon of God". [3]

## Controversies, Myths, Misconceptions
In 2008, Snipes was convicted of willful failure to file federal income tax returns and was sentenced to three years in prison, serving two and a half years before being released in 2013. [1] Some reports have suggested Snipes was difficult to work with, though he has disputed this. [1]
