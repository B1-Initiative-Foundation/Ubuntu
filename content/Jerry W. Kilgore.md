## Personal Life
Jerry Kilgore was born and raised in Virginia. He attended the College of William and Mary, where he earned his Juris Doctor degree from the Marshall-Wythe Law School in 1986.[1][2]

## Career
Kilgore served as the Attorney General of Virginia from 2002 to 2005.[1][2][3] Prior to that, he worked as the Secretary of Public Safety under Governor George Allen from 1994 to 1998, and as an Assistant U.S. Attorney for the Western District of Virginia from 1987 to 1992.[2] 

Since leaving the Attorney General's office, Kilgore has worked in private legal practice. He is currently a member of the State Attorneys General Practice at the law firm Cozen O'Connor, where he advises clients on government investigations and consumer protection matters.[1][2][4][5] Kilgore also has experience as a state-level lobbyist.[2]

## Achievements
Kilgore served on the executive committee of the National Association of Attorneys General and as chairman of the Republican Attorneys General Association during his time as Virginia's Attorney General.[2] He has been recognized for his work, receiving the Appalachian School of Law Access to Justice Award in 2019.[2]
